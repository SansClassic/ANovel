A Cursed Fantasy


Prologue — The End of Light

A man stood alone.

At his feet lay a mountain of demon corpses—charred, cleaved, crushed. A grotesque testament to his will.

But more came.

Always more.

Hordes of the damned surged over the blackened hills, clawing and howling, their eyes wild with bloodlust. Tireless. Endless.

The man gripped his sword tighter, shoulders rising with each labored breath.

“…There is no end to these vile beings…”

He could feel his body breaking—muscles screaming, bones cracking, vision blurring.

But he didn’t fall.

“I can’t give up… I won’t give up…”

And so he fought.

He fought as the sun rose and fell, as the moon bled light across a dying sky. Day after day. Night after night.

He fought until time became meaningless.

Until he was nothing but a blood-soaked echo of a warrior.

And still, the demons came.

Each swing of his blade cost more than the last. His footing faltered. His heartbeat slowed. His arms, once strong enough to fell titans, trembled.

He knew.

It wouldn’t be long now.

Another blow. Another scream. Another flash of steel.

His sword cracked.

Then broke.

And with it, so did the last hope of the world.

He dropped to one knee, surrounded.

“…If I fall… what happens next…?”

The man stood, breath ragged, as the demonic tide closed in—each twisted face drawing nearer with every heartbeat.

And in that moment, he remembered.

A promise.

A vow made long ago, when his hands were still clean and the world still believed in light.

A promise to save them… To end the reign of the Demon King… To fix what had been broken.

But now… now he stood at the edge.

Alone.

Bleeding.

Powerless.

“…I’m sorry… I’ve let you all do—”

He never finished the thought.

A clawed hand cracked across his skull—then another tore through his side.

And another.

And another.

...

The battlefield fell silent—except for the tearing of flesh and the distant echo of thunder.

Then, from beyond the mortal veil… voices stirred.

“The Champion has fallen…”

“…How could these mere insects allow this?”

A second voice, sharp with rage:

“Unforgivable. Punish them for such negligence.”

A third, venomous with scorn:

“They squandered our blessing… wallowed in greed while their protector bled.”

“Let them suffer.”

“No mercy. No salvation.”

Their words were not spoken—but decreed, like laws etched into the marrow of the world.

And thus it began.

The Curse of Humanity.
