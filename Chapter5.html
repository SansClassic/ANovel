Chapter 5 — Morality: Nothing More Than Words

Aina stepped out of the bar and into BrightVale.

She needed to grab something from her personal lab before heading to the meeting.

Once, this village had been bright. Warm. Alive.

Now it was a graveyard with roofs.

People were buried beside their own homes—makeshift graves marked by broken tools or worn shoes. Grief didn’t even sting anymore. What’s the point of mourning when death is just a few minutes away?

The town was quiet. Suffocatingly so.

Some villagers were lying down, trying to sleep off the hunger. Children—barely able to walk—still tried to play with rocks and sticks, smiling as though pretending could fill their bellies.

Then the village chief spotted her.

“Hey, Aina. Going out again?” He coughed. “Welp... I do hope you’re doing better than the rest of us.”

He tried to flash a smile, but it faltered halfway. “You’ve done everything you could. I know that. You didn’t sleep again last night, did you?”

Aina gave a tired shrug. “Yeah… not like I could sleep. I kept thinking, kept... trying to find something to fix this mess.”

The chief’s brows knit. “How long’s it been now?”

Aina rubbed her eyes. “Eh… a week maybe? Heh.”

“…A week of no sleep? Aina…”

“Can’t sleep, Chief. You know how it is…”

She kept walking.

Aina arrived at her home.

The door creaked softly open—and inside, warmth greeted her.

“Aina! You’re back!” Rein’s voice was bright as ever. “So how’d the village look? Is everyone okay?”

“Well... today’s checkup is still terrible,” she muttered. “But hey—could be worse.”

Rein gave a hopeful nod. “Yeah, I get that. Oh! Lydia said I could stay home today! Weird, right?”

Aina’s brow furrowed. “She did…? Did she say why?”

“Said I probably needed rest after working a whole month straight.”

Aina forced a chuckle. “That would be awesome... but maybe she’s right. You should rest. Just for today.”

“Okay! Thanks, Sis!”

Rein vanished into his room. Aina stood still, alone with her thoughts.

("...Something’s off.")

She stared at the closed door.

("Rein would never choose rest... Lydia did something.")

She shook it off. ("Whatever. What’s the worst that could happen…?")

Aina entered her lab. Dust, metal, broken dreams—three months of failed ideas.

She grabbed her schematics. Still incomplete.

Even now… nothing clicked. Nothing fit.

She sighed, rolled them up, and headed for the door—

“Wait!” Rein stepped into view. “You’re going out again? To where?”

“Ah, Lydia called me in for a meeting. Should be quick.”

“Alright. If anything happens, call me, okay?”

“Yeah, of course, Rein.”

“See you tonight, then!”

“Yeah… see you tonight.”

She stepped into the cold wind of BrightVale.

The skies were dark.

She entered the meeting chamber.

“Hey, Lydia,” she called, holding documents. “Got the reports—”

Silence.

“Uh… Lydia?”

“...Sorry, Aina.”

“Maybe… morality is no longer a choice.”

“Guards. Pin her.”

Rough hands slammed her to the floor.

“W-What’s going on?”

“Please… know this was never supposed to happen.”

“Your sacrifice… I’ll make sure it won’t be in vain.”

“No—wait!” Aina cried. “Just one more week! I swear!”

“What about my brother…?”

Footsteps. Amelia entered with the device.

“I made it… as fast as I could.”

Lydia took it. “Pick her up. Don’t let her move.”

The guards obeyed. Aina gasped.

The device neared—

Aina exploded with magic.

Guards flung back. Lydia staggered. The device clattered.

Aina stood—but legs failed. Still, she refused to fall.

“I see...” Lydia’s sword formed from light. “You had a bit of fight left.”

She struck. Aina dodged, barely.

“You’re not getting out of here.”

Another strike. Aina staggered. Cornered.

Her eyes met Lydia’s. She saw hesitation—maybe.

Crack.

Blade met temple.

Aina fell.

Blood pooled.

Lydia froze. (“Oh... what have I...”)

But her expression hardened. She stepped forward—

And lowered the device.

End of Chapter
